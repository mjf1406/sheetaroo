### Dictation Words

Compare: To look at two or more things and talk about how they are the same or how they are different.

Detail: A small piece of information that tells you more about a main idea or a story.

Identity: Who you are, including your name, your personality, and the things that make you special and different from others.

Feature: An important or interesting part of something (like the "features" on a new car or the "features" of a person's face).

Altogether: When you add everything up or look at the whole group; it means "completely" or "in total."

Culture: The way a group of people lives, including their traditions, food, music, language, and beliefs.

Signature: A person’s name written in their own way; it can also mean a special quality that makes something easily recognized as belonging to a specific person.

Main Idea: The most important point or the "big picture" message of a story or paragraph.

Subject Line: The short sentence at the top of an email that tells the reader what the email is about before they open it.
